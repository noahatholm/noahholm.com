I'm writing this two weeks after Nationals were played at Farnham Park. This has given me time to think about my season: what I enjoyed, what I didn't, and how I can improve for next year.

Background

This was my second year playing for my university team (the Nottingham Thieves); however, this was the first season where I consistently broke into the first team, starting every competitive game at first base.

We compete in the BUCS Baseball Midlands Tier 1, which includes: Nottingham 1's, Nottingham 2's, Cambridge 1's, East Anglia 1's, Loughborough 2's, and last but least, Loughborough 1's. All teams play each other once during the league phase, which spans from October to March (ideal baseball weather, I know :\\).

At the end of each season, a national tournament is played in early May at Farnham Park in beautiful Slough.

Farnham Park, Slough ^

The 12 best universities from the league phase qualify, which this year means the top 3 teams in each league. This is slightly different from the previous format of the top 2 + 2 wildcards due to the merging of the two northern leagues.

League Games

Nottingham 1's 22:4 Loughborough 2's

11th October 2025

Our first proper game of the season, following a friendly against the Long Eaton Storm, was played away at De Lisle College in Loughborough. It got off to a slow start, with us struggling to make meaningful contact, leading to a very even game going into the 4th inning. However, following a stern grilling from our coach, we bounced back to put up 14 runs across the next two innings, resulting in a final score of 22:4.

Nottingham 1's 12:1 East Anglia 1's

25th October 2025

Our second game was a grueling away day in Norwich against the runners-up at last year's Nationals. Fortunately, the sun came out just in time for the first pitch. The team played exceptionally well, winning 12:1. I was particularly proud of my performance in this game, picking up some good hits and finishing with a .750 batting average.

Nottingham 1's 2:6 Loughborough 1's

8th November 2025

Our third game was another away day, this time against our rivals L*ughborough. Defensively, we played an almost perfect game of baseball with zero errors; however, offensively, we struggled to bring runners home, regularly finishing innings with the bases loaded. We were punished for our lack of offense following a three-run home run that we just couldn't bounce back from. I was particularly disappointed with my own batting performance, finishing the day with 4 PA, 2 K's, and a .000 AVG.

This defeat was painful, but it gave us clear goals to work towards and improve upon.

Nottingham 2's 3:22 Nottingham 1's

14th February 2026

Following a three-month break for winter, we had our game against our own second team. Prior to this game, we'd had significant amounts of rain and the grass pitch was unusable, so it went ahead on the 3G football pitch we train on instead. This meant we had a comically short right field. There isn't much to say about this game; it was a chill friendly, which was basically ideal since I was recovering from a hangover.

I did actually get to pitch and close out the game, which was fun, though the pitching performance left a lot to be desired. I even hit someone on the shin, and was later informed they had to go to A&E for it. Safe to say I didn't pitch for the rest of the season 😔

Cambridge 1's 0:15 Nottingham 1's

15th March 2026

Going into our final game of the season, the league still had a lot of uncertainty since Cambridge and L*ughborough were still yet to play.

The game was rescheduled due to weather, which meant we were missing two of our starting infielders. However, the game went ahead on a miserable, rainy day.

We pulled off a dominant performance both defensively and offensively, holding them to zero runs while putting up 15 of our own.

I was happy with my performance in this game, finishing with a .750 AVG, 1.250 SLG, 2.000 OPS, and two stolen bases.

This was a great way to end the season with a 4-1 record. It would end up being enough to win the league, as Cambridge 1st beat L*ughborough 1st the following week, meaning we won the title based on run differential against the two other teams.

Italy Tour 🇮🇹

27th March - 31st March

Following on from our successful tour to Rome and Nettuno last year, the Nottingham Thieves returned to Italy, this time exploring Bologna and the Emilia-Romagna region.

We had the opportunity to play three friendly games against Pianoro, Castenaso, and a split squad layout combined with Pianoro against the Austrian Under-18s, as well as 3 training sessions.

The contrast of going from Nottingham's muddy Riverside Sports Complex to Castenaso under the Italian sun within two weeks was mind-blowing.

Overall, this was a great experience to play baseball with my friends, exchange culture with the opposing teams, and explore the Bologna nightlife on a random Monday in March.

Nationals

2nd May - 3rd May

Thanks to topping our division, we headed into Nationals pretty confident. Our group consisted of UCL, Cardiff, and Edinburgh—all teams we believed we could beat.

Nottingham 1's 7:8 UCL

This was a great, competitive game of baseball. We put up six runs in the first inning while holding them to four, but after that, our production slowed down, leading to a 6:6 tie going into a scoreless third. Entering the fifth and final inning, we trailed by one run but managed to tie it up; unfortunately, we were walked off in the bottom half to lose 7:8, heartbreaking.

Despite the loss, it was a really fun game that honestly could have gone either way. Looking back, I do believe our pitching strategy could have been better optimised to tip the margins in our favor. We threw our third choice pitcher to save our top two arms for Sunday. However, since this was our most challenging matchup of the day, utilising our aces for even a brief 30-pitch relief stint likely would have been enough to change the outcome.

Personally, I played decent finishing with a 0 errors, .500 AVG, and a 1.167 OPS.

Nottingham 1's 20:1 Cardiff

This matchup was a stark contrast to the great game of baseball we played against UCL. Cardiff was the weakest team in their division, only qualifying due to a general lack of competitiveness within their league. To add to their troubles, they were missing their two best pitchers. Consequently, the game devolved into a slow-pitch softball "walk-fest" on offense, but resulted in a dominant shutout, no-hitter performance on defense. This was because we started our best pitcher, saving his 30-pitch limit specifically for this matchup.

I only played the first two innings of this game before being substituted to allow for squad rotations. Overall, this game was a drag to play and watch; the once-sunny sky had been replaced with overcast clouds, putting a disappointing end to Saturday despite the win.

Edinburgh 4:3 Nottingham 1's

Following our defeat to UCL on Saturday and UCL's victory against Edinburgh, qualifying for the semifinals looked unlikely. However, we still needed to win the game in front of us to even have a chance.

Edinburgh started hot, putting up three runs on us in the first inning, including a two-run home run. We failed to respond early, leaving the score at 3:0 going into the third.

At the start of the third, a strange incident occurred: an off-duty umpire watching the game from behind the stands was attempting to call the game, which led to a confrontation and the eventual ejection of our head coach. Surprisingly, this drama gave us a massive boost in morale, and we rallied to put up three runs over the next two innings. Unfortunately, a series of defensive errors at second base later allowed the deciding run to cross the plate for Edinburgh.

Despite the result, it was an enjoyable game of baseball that had its ups and downs, but ultimately, the finest of margins just didn't go our way.

Analysis

I finished Nationals with a perfect defensive record, posting a 1.000 FP and turning one double play. However, offensively, I failed to hit the ball for extra bases. Despite finishing with a .500 AVG, my SLG was also just .500.

This pattern was more or less consistent across the entire team. Defensively, the team put up a .891 FP (which rises to .956 if you omit the three second-base errors against Edinburgh) alongside a 9.00 ERA (5th best in the tournament). Clearly, defense was not our problem. In the batter's box, we boasted a team line of a .392 AVG, .608 SLG, and a 1.221 OPS. While this shows our contact wasn't bad, we also drew the highest number of walks per game in the competition, which reveals a clear lack of confidence at the plate.

In a way, Nationals served as a perfect microcosm of our season: slow to start, incredibly solid defensively (for the most part), and lacking aggression in the batter's box.

Hindsight is, of course, 20/20. You can always point to a different pitching strategy, better umpiring, or how a mere two extra runs against UCL and Edinburgh completely would have rewritten the narrative. That said, I'm a firm believer that you make your own luck. Our hesitancy to swing the bat and being content with a walk directly led to us underperforming across the majority of the season.

Going into next year, we are losing a lot of senior players.Hence, recruitment and transitioning our second team and bench players into the starting lineup will be our top priorities, alongside instilling an aggressive mindset to avoid the same pitfalls next season.
